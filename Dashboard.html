Learning Etiquette (3Ps)

Be Punctual

Arrive on time for your lesson.

Be Prepared

Have you practiced the assigned pieces?
Is your homework completed?
Did you bring all necessary materials - books, stationery, small notebook?

Be Proactive

Do you ask questions when uncertain?
Have you informed your absence due to illness at least a day in advance?
Have you promptly informed of any holiday plans?

Practice Habits

Practice Regularly

Aim for daily practice, minimum 20 minutes each time.

Warm Up

Begin with scales & arpeggios or finger exercises to loosen your fingers.

Set Clear Goals

Always focus on mastering a specific section or technical skills before moving on.
Scales & Arpeggios: notes & key, fingerings & turns, dynamic consistency, tempo consistency, ...
Sight-reading: note reading, interval reading, tactile sense, rhythmic sense, ...
Pieces: break into smaller sections and work through notes, fingerings, rhythm, articulation, ... slowly
Aural: clapping, rhythmic sense, pitch recognition, aural identification of musical elements, ...
